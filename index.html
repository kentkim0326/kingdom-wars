Visual Identity

Midjourney-inspired AI warship art, planet tile maps, nation flags on every captured sector, and battle results presented as cinematic 2D playback.

Game Overview

The core fantasy is simple and powerful: a real-world nation fights for solar-system territory, builds fleets, and earns in-game value through conquest, strategy, and creator activity.

Core Concept

A mobile-first 2D space metaverse where each player represents a real nation and competes for sectors across the Moon, Mars, Jupiter moons, and Saturn moons.

Positioning

The project frames itself as a metaverse utility economy, not a gambling product, combining P2E, nation pride, and a creator marketplace.

Player Promise

Fast onboarding, turn-based strategy, low stress combat, and meaningful progression through territory control and collectible units.

World Lore

Setting

The year is 2387. Earth’s resources are exhausted, the United Earth Council has collapsed, and nations now extend their rivalry into space. The Moon becomes the first battlefield, followed by Mars and the outer planets.

Planet Progression

Each season introduces a new planetary theater with distinct resources, map sizes, and strategic pacing, which keeps the meta fresh and opens new expansion milestones.

Gameplay Loop

Territory

Owned sectors generate resources every turn and become the backbone of expansion, defense, and economic scaling.

Units

Players spend resources to build and upgrade warships and mobile suits, balancing offense, defense, and utility synergies.

Battle

Fleet deployment leads into auto-resolved battles with visual playback, keeping strategy deep without forcing real-time stress.

Turn Structure

  • Strategy phase: inspect map and choose target sectors.
  • Deploy phase: place units in the battle grid.
  • Resolution phase: auto-battle plays out in 30 to 60 seconds.
  • Result phase: territory, resources, and KDW rewards update immediately.

Battle Identity

Every match feels like a national war story, with flags, ship skins, mobile suits, and victory screens reinforcing player identity and long-term attachment.

KDW Tokenomics

KDW is the game’s utility token on Base Network, with ERC-20 compatibility, fixed total supply, and multiple sinks tied to gameplay and the marketplace.

Token Specs

Total supply: 1,000,000,000 KDW. DEX integration is built around OmniSwap, and the ecosystem also includes OMNI/MIL connectivity inside the broader Milliverse framework.

Economic Logic

Rewards come from territory occupation, battle victories, season rankings, guild dominion, marketplace sales, and tournaments, while utility flows through purchases, upgrades, staking, and burns.

Earning Methods

Sector occupation, battle victory bonuses, season leaderboard payouts, guild split rewards, marketplace income, and tournament prizes all feed the reward economy.

Utility Sinks

Warship purchases, upgrades, creator registration fees, mothership construction, planet expansion, staking, and transaction burns keep KDW circulating.

Burn Mechanism

Two percent of marketplace KDW transactions are burned, creating a built-in scarcity mechanic that supports long-term token discipline.

Creator Marketplace

The marketplace is modeled like an internal Steam or App Store, letting creators design warship and mobile-suit skins, register them, and sell them for KDW.

Creator Flow

Creators use AI tools such as Midjourney to generate assets, register them on-chain or in the game economy, and earn from direct sales and recurring engagement.

Revenue Model

The platform collects registration fees, takes a 30% transaction commission, and also sells official premium packs, flagship ships, and season passes.

Technical Stack

Frontend

Next.js PWA for mobile-first access without app-store friction at launch.

Backend

Supabase for auth, storage, and database operations, matching the game’s existing ecosystem approach.

Blockchain

Base Network ERC-20 architecture for KDW with low-cost transactions and Ethereum security.

Battle Engine

Server-side TypeScript handles deterministic, cheat-resistant battle resolution so outcomes are reproducible and fair.

Supabase Storage and CDN host warship art and marketplace assets, while AI image workflows accelerate content production.
